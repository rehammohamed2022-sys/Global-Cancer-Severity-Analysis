# Global Cancer Severity Analysis

## Econometric Modeling and Statistical Analysis Project

### Overview

Cancer remains one of the leading causes of morbidity and mortality worldwide, with disease severity influenced by demographic, environmental, clinical, and lifestyle-related factors.

This project applies econometric and statistical modeling techniques to investigate the determinants of cancer severity using an international dataset covering cancer patients from 2015 to 2024. The analysis examines how cancer stage, genetic risk, air pollution, obesity, smoking, alcohol consumption, treatment costs, and demographic characteristics contribute to variations in cancer severity.

---

## Research Objectives

* Identify the major determinants of cancer severity.
* Examine differences in cancer severity across cancer stages and demographic groups.
* Evaluate the impact of genetic, environmental, and lifestyle risk factors.
* Compare alternative econometric models and interaction effects.
* Assess model assumptions and diagnostic validity.
* Generate evidence-based insights for public health research and policy.

## Key Findings

### Cancer Severity Determinants

* Cancer severity increased significantly with advanced cancer stages.
* Genetic risk was one of the strongest predictors of disease severity.
* Air pollution showed a significant positive association with severity outcomes.
* Higher treatment costs were associated with more severe cancer cases.

### Lifestyle and Health Factors

* Smoking was identified as the strongest lifestyle-related predictor of cancer severity.
* Alcohol consumption significantly increased severity scores.
* Obesity contributed positively to disease progression and severity.

### Demographic Effects

* Older patients tended to experience higher severity levels.
* Male patients exhibited higher average severity scores than female patients.
